    Project Idea - Payment Tracker

    Overview

    When going on holiday with friends/family, going out to eat or attending an event it can typically become difficult to track all the payments made, specifically when someone has loaned money. My project idea is to create an application which tracks when you owe ‘x’ money and when ‘x’ owes you money. Rather than going on holiday and having to constantly transfer money to multiple recipients the group can track all transactions and pay in which manner suits them best.

    Motivation

    The motivation for this project idea came from work colleagues and friends. When working with colleagues and going away/out with friends we often purchase items such as coffee, lunch, dinner, entertainment and snacks for each other daily, by doing this we are having to constantly transfer funds on a daily basis. Transferring funds as often as this, floods your transaction list with little payments. Therefore, the need for an app, which simply lists how much each party member has loaned to ‘x’.

    Description

    When budgeting and keeping track of payments these little payments can add up unnoticeably and are difficult to track. This app allows you to keep track of how much money each member borrowed or has loaned out. This way the party in which owes money can pay in the manner that is most suitable to them. Or can track how much they have been loaning out. As an example, you are going away with friends on a holiday. A title can be created ‘Spain Holiday 2022’ and a new group inside for each member of the holiday. Within each member details on how much they owe and how much they are owed is listed. This is also detailed so they know who owes them money and who they owe money.

    • The holiday has ended and now Joe can pay Sarah the $300 he owes her from the multiple times in which Sarah loaned Joe money, rather than having to pay the $300 throughout the holiday. One simple transaction can be made described as ‘Spain Holiday 2022’. This avoids any scenario which may include the times both parties forgot Sarah loaned money to Joe and avoid the simple little transactions, each time having to enter a description of the payment. Using the scenario provided earlier with the work colleagues and friends, you see these people daily and lend money when paying for lunch or dinner. Rather than requesting your work colleague or friend to transfer money on the occasion, you can document it and pay at a later date. An example, if your work colleague goes out to buy you lunch often it is easier to document it and pay them in bulk rather than pay them $15 everyday. As version 2 of the application and later, other features are to be added such as paying the member through the app. Rather than having to enter the app to determine how much it is required to pay and proceeding by having to open your bank app and manually type the amount. Other version 2 or later features include setting day limits to which you must pay the recipient, an example if Sarah and Joe worked together and they frequently purchased each other lunch and drinks, Sarah can request to have the loaned money paid within 30 days. As well as adding a system to calculate how much money you have borrowed from said ‘user’ and how much you have loaned out. The application will be accessible via IOS, MacOS, Android and Windows.

    Tools and Technology

    Software for development: ReactNative will be used to develop the application, as it provides capability to be accessible on all platforms required. Database: As data will need to be stored from the users input and users will need to be distinguished a noSQL database will be used, specifically Firebase as this database will work smoothly with ReactNative. Further benefit to using Firebase, it acts in real-time therefore all updates made by users will be uploaded to all other users In real-time. Servers: As we are developing the app with Firebase a cloud service, we will not require any servers.

    Skills Required

  • Programming ability with Java (required for React Native).
  • Understanding of cloud infrastructure in order to work with Firebase.
  • Ability to simultaneously translate between programming for each operating system.
  • Ability to maintain and design databases to ensure a smooth and well performing app.

    • These skills can be obtained through researching the software and understanding what you need specifically with each component as your needs with React Native and Firebase will be different to other users, although the further versions of the application will begin to require a more expert level of knowledge. This is also true for the programming aspect however, the ability to program an application with the flexibility that is required will not be an easy skill to fine.

    Outcome

    If the project Is successful an application will be developed in which allows users to have a simple way of monitoring and tracking borrowed money by friends and family on holidays, work occasions or simply going out on the weekend. The initial problem of cluttered transactions due to constantly transferring peers’ money will be solved by now having a platform which you can keep track of these transactions with friends and pay borrowed money back at a later date. The impact of the development will mean friends and family can go on holiday or go out for lunch and enjoy their time without worrying about constantly transferring money for a small drink or meal every time.
